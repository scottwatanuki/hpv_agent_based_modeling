HPV Agent-Based Modeling

Project summary, team, and links to outputs

Summary

We built a lightweight agent-based model (ABM) of Human Papillomavirus (HPV) transmission tailored to the Atlanta Metropolitan Area to evaluate vaccination strategies. Using the open-source HPVsim framework, the model simulates individual demographics, sexual partnerships, age-specific routine and catch-up vaccination, per-partnership transmission, and natural clearance. We calibrated key elements with U.S. Census and CDC sources and ran scenario analyses varying vaccine coverage and efficacy to estimate reductions in HPV prevalence and incident infections. The codebase and report highlight policy-relevant insights for local public health planning.

Key results

Scenario analyses indicate that higher vaccination coverage and earlier, pre-exposure vaccination reduce modeled HPV prevalence and incident infections in Atlanta-like populations. Catch-up vaccination further lowers transmission in the short-to-medium term, with overall impact shaped by sexual mixing heterogeneity and vaccine efficacy. While cancer progression was not explicitly modeled, reductions in persistent infection translate to expected long-run prevention benefits; we provide a transparent, reproducible framework and policy-oriented discussion in the accompanying report.
